My Latest Activities

Program Leader of Program HUMAIN (Human-Macaque Interface)

HUMAIN (Human-macaque interface) Program is a collaborative research and conservation project of the University of the Philippines Mindanao, UP Visayas Tacloban College, University of Southern Mindanao, and Visayas State University. It aims to study the behavior and diet preferences of long-tailed macaques in selected human modified landscapes of Leyte and Mindanao islands.

See more research and conservation

Publication: Predation on a Parachute Gecko Gekko sp. by Long-tailed Macaques Macaca fascicularis (Raffles, 1821) in Mt. Apo Natural Park, Philippines

Long-tailed macaques are omnivores feeding on plants and animal matter. They are known to feed on meat occasionally, and it represents a very small portion of their diet. This paper reports a predation event by long-tailed macaques on a parachute gecko in Mt. Apo Natural Park, Philippines.
